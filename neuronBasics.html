Neuron Basics

A Neural Network works on the principle of simulating a real-world set of neurons. A neuron works naturally in the brain by sending electrical signals that connect all neurons together. Once a single neuron sends a signal it creates a wave or butterfly effect outwards to other neurons. A neuron acts only when a signal is strong enough for it to also activate, for example if a neuron is to send a signal and it is strong enough to activate the next, a signal will be sent. This is known as a weight. In a neural network the weights are calibrated or "Trained" to give a specific end result at the output layer.
